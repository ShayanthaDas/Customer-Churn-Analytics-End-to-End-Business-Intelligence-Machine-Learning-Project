# Customer Churn Analytics Case Study

## Executive Summary

A telecommunications company observed increasing customer attrition over the past year. Management wanted to understand why customers were leaving and how data analytics could support retention efforts.

This case study demonstrates how analytics, business intelligence, and machine learning were used to uncover churn patterns and develop actionable business recommendations.

---

## Background

Customer retention is a major concern in the telecommunications industry. While attracting new customers requires significant marketing expenditure, retaining existing customers is generally more cost-effective.

The company sought a comprehensive analysis of customer behavior to identify the factors influencing churn.

---

## Problem Statement

The organization faced three key challenges:

1. Rising customer churn rates.
2. Limited visibility into customer behavior.
3. Lack of predictive insights for retention planning.

Management required a data-driven solution capable of identifying churn drivers and forecasting customer attrition.

---

## Analytical Approach

The project followed a structured analytics framework:

### Stage 1: Data Preparation

The dataset was cleaned and validated using Microsoft Excel and Python.

Activities included:

* Handling missing values
* Correcting data types
* Creating calculated variables
* Preparing data for analysis

---

### Stage 2: Exploratory Data Analysis

Customer behavior patterns were explored using Python.

Key focus areas:

* Churn distribution
* Contract analysis
* Payment behavior
* Revenue analysis
* Customer tenure analysis

Several visualizations highlighted important relationships between customer characteristics and churn outcomes.

---

### Stage 3: SQL Analytics

Business-oriented analysis was conducted using MySQL and PostgreSQL.

Key analyses included:

* Customer segmentation
* Revenue contribution analysis
* Churn rate calculations
* Contract performance analysis
* Risk scoring

Advanced PostgreSQL functions were used to perform statistical and cohort analyses.

---

### Stage 4: Machine Learning

An XGBoost classification model was developed to predict customer churn.

The model evaluated:

* Contract type
* Monthly charges
* Customer tenure
* Service subscriptions
* Customer value indicators

The resulting model identified customers with elevated churn risk.

---

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: Contract Type Matters

Customers on month-to-month contracts showed significantly higher churn rates compared with customers on annual or multi-year contracts.

### Finding 2: New Customers Are More Vulnerable

Customers with short tenure were considerably more likely to leave than long-term customers.

### Finding 3: Monthly Charges Influence Retention

Higher monthly charges were associated with increased churn risk.

### Finding 4: Support Services Improve Loyalty

Customers utilizing technical support and security services demonstrated stronger retention.

---

## Business Impact

Based on these findings, management can:

* Reduce customer attrition.
* Improve customer lifetime value.
* Increase retention campaign effectiveness.
* Prioritize high-risk customer groups.
* Optimize customer engagement strategies.

---

## Recommendations

### Short-Term

* Launch retention offers for month-to-month customers.
* Engage customers during their first year.
* Improve onboarding experiences.

### Medium-Term

* Promote annual contracts.
* Introduce loyalty incentives.
* Expand customer support services.

### Long-Term

* Deploy predictive churn monitoring.
* Integrate machine learning into CRM systems.
* Establish a customer retention analytics framework.

---

## Conclusion

This project demonstrates how data analytics can transform raw customer data into actionable business intelligence. By combining Excel, SQL, Python, machine learning, and business intelligence tools, organizations can proactively identify churn risks and implement targeted retention strategies.

The approach not only improves operational decision-making but also supports long-term customer relationship management and revenue growth.
